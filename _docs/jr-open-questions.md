## 4) Two you asked me to explain

- **What "registering your brand" means (A2P 10DLC).** It's an Intulse/mobile-carrier thing, **not** a government filing. "Brand" just means your business identity — legal name, EIN, address — submitted to an industry registry (The Campaign Registry) so the phone carriers know who's behind the texts. "Campaign" is a short description of what your texts are and how people opt in. Carriers now block business texts from anyone who isn't registered, which is the spam crackdown you've probably heard about. The reason they want a real website with a privacy policy and terms is that the carriers actually check that a matching, legitimate business site exists before they approve you. So the website unlocks the texting, exactly as Intulse told you.
- **Your Google/Yelp reviews.** Yes, to be safe it's worth adding a short line to your reviews like *"Full disclosure: I volunteered to build their website, with no quid pro quo."* That's an FTC honesty thing (disclosing a relationship). We'll also keep your reviews out of the cherry-picked set on the site, and hold off on linking the Yelp profile until there's at least one other Yelp review besides yours.
