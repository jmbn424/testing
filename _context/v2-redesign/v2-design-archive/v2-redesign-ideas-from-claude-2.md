**On code fences in prompts:** no meaningful effect on understanding, and this session is the proof, since your fenced instructions were executed exactly as intended. I read fenced and unfenced text the same way. Where fences genuinely help: clean boundaries between your words and quoted material, preserved whitespace, and a signal that content is verbatim. The one place they can mislead is ambiguity of intent, where a fenced block could read as sample text to reuse rather than an instruction to follow. When that ambiguity is possible, a one-word lead-in like "run:" or "for reference:" removes it entirely. Paste from VS Code freely.
