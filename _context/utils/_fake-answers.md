## Answers from J&R

**Where the information goes (the anchor).**
When someone fills out the form, it goes into AgencyBloc and stays with us. We don't sell leads and we don't share them with other agencies for their marketing. When we can't serve someone, say they're in a state we're not licensed in, we point them toward someone who can, we don't hand over their details. Advocate does send us recycled and cold leads, so their data comes to us, but we don't send our clients' information back to them.

**Paying for leads.**
Our leads are word-of-mouth, our Medicare 101 content, and the occasional organic referral. We don't buy lead lists or pay per lead.

**Legal name and DBA.**
"J&R Health Insurance LLC" is the registered entity, and "J&R Health Insurance" is the name we trade under.

**State licenses.**
We're licensed and active in Washington, Oregon, Georgia, and Tennessee, none lapsed. California and Nevada are in progress.

**Vetecare Certified.**
"It's a certification we earn through Advocate's veterans program. It is not VA accreditation and we don't do VA claims work. It only shows up with our Veterans services content.

**PHI in operations.**
When we're servicing a client with a carrier, yes, we handle some of their health-plan information. The website itself never touches it.

No, they dont' require this.

**AgencyBloc retention and recordings.**
AgencyBloc lets us manage retention. Whether it can run two separate clocks we'd have to check. And yes, we use Scopes of Appointment and our calls are handled per CMS rules.
